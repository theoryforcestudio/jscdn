The subtle art of leeching
by ~S~ nn, January 2000
Slightly edited by fravia+


OK, and sorry for my crap-english, here: fravia+ has invited me to held today's workshop as a "Master leecher"... a title which could appear almost as an insult to some unaware readers ;-] Yet I'll try to explain you WHY it is [at times] important to leech and WHERE you should eventually practicize this art if you are so inclined.
I'm sure you are aware of the fact that professional leeching is a science in its own rights, with great and well-known historical traditions. It is currently practicized [at times with considerable mastership] in all universities, political parties, corporations, financial advisors companies, newspapers and advertisement agencies I know of, to cite but a few of the places where you are 100% sure to meet some great leechers.

The web, through its pseudo-anonymity and thanks to the naïvität of many good souls, who are indeed offering real knowledge for free, has obviously incredibly increased all kind of leeching possibilities. You can be sure that on any site, messageboard, newsgroup where people that 'are in the know' are exchanging some snippets of real information there are dozens of 'professional' leechers overing around.
I will not enter now into the curious and arkane world of newsgroup and messageboard tricks & lures, I'm sure fravia+ himself and other well known avatars know more that enough about such stuff ;-] therefore they may explain those things themselves [if they care] much better than I ever could, but I'll tell you right away some principle that you should IMO head if you want to master the subtle art of leeching [or should you aspire to a "Master Leecher" title as well ;-] which I believe you should by all means for reasons that I hope will be clear to you before you have finished reading this short essay.

Let's try to list some principles:
  • Real leeching is difficult: you must constantly resist to the temptation to interfer with the people you are targeting. Real leechers NEVER really emerge to the surface. In fact I suspect that there are even 'leechers hunters' out there [like the owner of this site? ;-] that will try to arpion you out the very moment you lower your shields [that's actually what happened to me, thus I'm writing this against all leechers' principles ;-]
  • Leeching is "immaterial" [I'm not sure that this concept exactly mirrors what I mean], you gain a significant amount of knowledge without being involved in any of the tedious discussions that afflicts newsgroups and messageboards. Your [considerable] advantage is that you know precisely where you took what, others will have to search a lot to understand and demonstrate even a small part of that. The advantages and gains that this offers are obvious.
  • Far from being totally separate from the observation field, good leechers can even try to "direct" their targets. If you feel it is really necessary, in order to underline a point or to gain a better understanding of a specific question, a quick intervention by a faked pseudo [to be discarded afterwards and never used again] can work wonders on any newsgroup. Compare this with the difficulty that you would have in order to obtain the same informations with a "known" avatar: "Hey, how comes you don't know this?".
  • Remember, though, that you'll have to have at least some elementary trolling knowledge to direct targets correctly. Lower profile and naïvität work wonders in this field.
  • Least but not last, you will have to develop the ability to QUICKLY recognize the value of the information fonts you are leeching from. Nothing is more disturbing for a leecher to constate afterwards that the material he has laboursomely leeched was after all not so good. You will have to master the art of FINDING QUICKLY the ABSOLUTE TOP-LEVEL fonts of information about your target in order to leech effectively. Master leechers know how to recognize a flake from a mile distance, indipendently from all the professional 'hype' he may have used to smoke his site and his material...
I'll now try to explain with some examples how IMPORTANT leeching can be for whatever cause you happen to fight for ;-]
Leeching is most of the time directed towards your [real or supposed] "enemies". Let's say for instance that you are trying to fight the spammers that use all tricks of the trade in order to put their commercial shit sites in the first slots of the main search engines... well, in that case you would be well advised to leech [certainly not contribute!] their 'dedicated' messageboards, like, for instance: http://searchengineforums.com/bin/Ultimate.cgi
You had a look? Impressive stuff, isn't it? Ideal field for brave leechers!
