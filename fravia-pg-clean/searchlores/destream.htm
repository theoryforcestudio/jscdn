Introduction

In today's internet media plays a large role, from listening to a favorite radio station or watching the trailer of an up and coming movie, the chances are that everyone with an internet connection has had their lives touched in one way or another by digital media. This in itself isn't a bad thing, the problem arises when you find your movies and audio in a proprietary format which locks you into a single player or the media is only playable online. When forced into using one of these players, you'll often find you also get some other goodies. These are normally just bundled software intent on malbehaving which is bad enough, but some serious threats to your computer have arose in these players. Withheld in these pages and essays is your freedom from this.

Still Image Tools

The general misconception is that images are harmless when it comes to privacy, unless you happen to take a picture of yourself holding a card with your name and address on it. The truth is that images can contain many extra details. For instance your digital camera stores information on you. Also some web sites, mainly pr0n web sites write EXIF data when you download your pictures. This data uniquely identifies you with your account, so if they see it available for download on usenet or file sharing networks they will close your account and perhaps prosecute. With all that in mind, the other reason to use these tools is to remove the crud that app's such as photoshop write into files.

Threats

DRM (Digital Rights Management)

The most prominent threat to media of any kind. Microsoft has a page explaining it in more detail here, the EFF has it's own page here. But in short, DRM aims to put an end to the sharing of media, not only on PC's and portable devices but also on HDTV's. The next step after that would be to get it into stereo/Hi-Fi equipment and stop the sharing of CDs. This technology is already being used by online content providers, the theory is that you get to watch a movie but only on operating systems that support windows media player 9. You purchase a movie, download it to your local computer. When you try to watch the movie WMP9 connects to a server to authenticate your password, if this is accepted the movie will on the fly decrypt. Some of the more tasteless readers may have seen this method used to distribute the Paris Hilton tape. Message boards were flooded with people asking how to convert the movie they had downloaded but not paid for, or in other cases were they had paid for it but it was limited to only a certain amount of views before it corrupted itself. These files are nigh on impossible to convert, locking out users of non MS operating systems and requiring you to be online when you view them.

Trusted Computing

The corporate wet dream of Microsoft, Intel, IBM, HP and AMD. Originally developed for DRM purposes, it soon escalated into a full fledged 'protection' system for computing platforms. Trusted computing aims to put your PC into the hands of other people, kind of like a spyware gone mad. An in depth look into what TC attempts to do can be found here.
