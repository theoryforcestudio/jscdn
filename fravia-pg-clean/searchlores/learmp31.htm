Fravia.. looking at your new searching site has inspired me to actually write down how I have searched for something. I do not think I am a great searcher, the trade is just too easy to be elitist! But I had already chewed much of the material found on your site (or is there an 'experienced' section?).
So, I assume this little 'lesson' may be beneficial to those new at searching.

I'm sure you would never search for my particular target, but I was looking for a place to download mp3s from a little known heavy metal/techno band MDFMK. I did what you suggested which was to write down what I am looking for and time myself a maximum amount of 15 minutes. I completed this search in 9:54 minutes!

Searching for mp3s is nowadays one of the most common searches. Normally, I don't even consider this kind of retrieval a real "search": I just logon to [Napster], and look for the songs, which *normally* are always there. But, this time I had no luck: out of over 200,000 songs, and 1 terabyte of other songs.. I could not find any MDFMK songs at all! :(

Well I have now found napster, which I recommend! You get there around a terabyte (or 1000GBs) of songs all the time: no more broken links, no more slow downloads, and no more busy, disorganized FTP sites... Anyway, before Napster I looked for music either on IRC, or Homemade FTPs.
I DO NOT RECCOMEND GETTING SONGS THROUGH THESE TWO PATHS!! They can be used only as a last resort.
On IRC, simply go into one of the huge mp3 channels (cablemp3) and use @locator <song/band/ect.>, then follow the bots instructions. For the ftps, I recommend you use CuteFTP or another FTP program, search for the song/band at one of those mp3 FTP depositories (such as mp3.box.sk, mp3search.lycos.com, www.audiogalaxy.com, www.oth.net, and I suppose http://ftpsearch.lycos.com) from there, it's a guessing game normally, to see which FTPs are not busy, have ratios, have been taken down, need you to survive a barrage of banner clicking... its a hell out there! So, go there as a LAST resort!! Ok.. this is how I started out..

Naspter's search for MDFMK gave me... nothing!! Now I had to actually work for something, this is when I started the clock :)

This is my recommendation for finding a song in MP3 form:

  • 1) Search in Napster.
  • 2) Look in the bands USENET forum. (alt.music.<bandhere>)
  • 3) Search the engines.
  • 4) Look in the 'homemade FTPs'
  • 5) Look in IRC

    and also, Note that I am sure that ICQ has stuff like that as well, but I'm not into it, so at eventual 6th place, search inside ICQ :)

    I hoped this has helped anyone to find mp3s, remember as always, to buy the CD after, if you like the band, support them!
